Chapter One

The Awakening

You don't know how you got here. Your head is pounding. But one by one, the senses return.

A strange humming sound overloads your senses. The vibration resonates in your body, startling you from a deep slumber. You open your eyes — vision blurry, room ill-lit, reeking of dry sweat and cold fog.

Light filters through a small circular hole in the ceiling. As awareness sharpens, you realize you are restrained in a large metal cradle — arms, waist, ankles, and neck all strapped in place. Clear flesh-like tubes run along the ceiling in every direction, ending in what appear to be serpent fangs puncturing your left forearm just below the elbow.

You are not alone. Seven cradles. Seven figures. All waking.

And then a voice — nervous, urgent — breaks the silence: "I need to get out of here… they need to get out of here!"

With a wet splat, all seven tubes drop through the hole and hang swaying in the center of the chamber, dripping a thick residue onto immaculately carved stone. Strange runes and glyphs cover the floor where the light falls brightest. Behind each cradle sits a wooden chest. Your equipment — and your answers — wait to be found.

What lies beyond the chamber

  • The Corridor LabA wall of morgue drawers and operating tables. Every body: a dead changeling, each bearing a number tattoo in arcane ink. In the corner — jars of mind flayer parasite fragments, some whole and intact.
  • The Mass GraveA spiral staircase leads down into a vast open cavern. Hundreds of changeling bodies in various stages of decomposition. All numbered. All silent. The scale of what was done here is beyond comprehension.
  • The SurfaceA hatch opens into a great arched boulder. Nearby, the circular well-like ring above the chamber. Beyond it: a lush tropical meadow, then dense forest stretching in all directions. They are somewhere else entirely.

The Party

The Gifted

The experiment altered each of them in ways not yet understood — six survivors, six gifts, and a seventh seat left empty. They will teeter on the edge of believing themselves poisoned or cursed — before the truth reveals itself as something far stranger. Something gifted.

Roscoe Tealeaf
Halfling · Sorlock · Aberrant Mind / Dao Genie
Gift: Telepath

Born to wandering tellers and dreamers. His Dao pact awakened something older — an alien whisper in his mind. "Most minds are closed books. I prefer the ones with loose pages."

  • Memory Maze Enter a creature's mind as a bonus action and witness fragments of their recent memories — unbeknownst to them. Recharges on long rest
  • Altered Motive Push a thought into a creature's mind. On a failed Wisdom save, it follows your motive for up to one minute. It won't harm loved ones or break sacred oaths. Recharges on long rest

Early sign: A nervously tense voice mutters — "I need to get out of here… they need to get out of here!" The words seem to come from nearby. Or perhaps from inside your own skull.

Born beneath canvas roofs and starlit skies, Roscoe Tealeaf never knew a permanent home. His family belonged to one of the great wandering caravans of the Sword Coast — fortune tellers, musicians, tinkers, gamblers, and dreamers. A silver tongue could earn a meal, a clever lie could avoid a fight.

Even as a child, he heard whispers others could not. Fleeting thoughts brushed against his mind like distant conversations heard through a wall. His family dismissed it as fey blood. Roscoe accepted their explanations, though the occurrences grew stronger every year.

While sheltering from a storm in ruined manor deep in Cormyr, he discovered an ornate brass lantern beneath a collapsed altar — gleaming as though freshly polished. The moment his fingers touched it, a voice echoed inside his mind: "At last..."

Tahzir of the Endless Caverns — a Dao genie imprisoned centuries ago — offered knowledge, power, and answers. The pact awakened something buried deep in Roscoe's soul. He learned to project thoughts, twist spells through sheer will, and glimpse possibilities hidden from ordinary sight. Yet he has begun to suspect Tahzir is withholding important truths. Why was he imprisoned? And by whom?

Last memory before the experiment: "In the shadow of that evening you remember seeing a shimmering blue light fall from the sky in the distance. The memory is blurry at first but returns as you focus on it. To you it seemed to fall at the pace of a snowflake, gracefully slow. Captivated by its beauty you took a moment to appreciate this rare anomaly. Then you heard a snap, turned around, and were immediately impacted with a wave of sky blue light that quickly turned white and blinded you. You remember hearing an exhausted, breathy sigh before falling into the most relaxed state of bliss you have ever known. Then darkness."
Drogath Ironbound
Orc · Fighter · Polearm Master
Gift: Fortitude

A warrior forged in arenas and battlefields, carrying an oath older than his freedom. The Butcher of Blackstone fights not for glory — for vengeance. For the father who held the gate.

  • Tower Double your size and all gear for 3 minutes. Gain +1d8 on attacks, advantage on Strength, doubled jump height, +10ft movement. Recharges on long rest
  • Stronghold Transform skin into natural plate armor for 1 minute. Gain +2 AC and resistance to piercing damage. Recharges on long rest

Early sign: As you open and close your hands you notice a tightness. The back of your hands look dark and weathered — like leather. You make a fist and feel the tough hide on your knuckles struggle to stretch. Shooting pain runs deep into your hands.

Drogath remembers only fragments of the day the Stonejaw Clan fell. The smell of smoke. The clash of steel against the mountain gate. His mother's hand shoving him toward the safety tunnels. And his father — Gorun Ironbound, Gatekeeper of Stonejaw Pass — standing atop the shattered walls with a glaive in hand.

For generations, the Ironbound family had defended the narrow mountain road connecting isolated settlements. Their clan believed strength was measured not by conquest, but by the lives protected behind their walls. When the attackers came, Gorun refused to abandon his post. Drogath never saw him fall.

Still a child, he was chained and sold to the masters of the Blackstone Arena, who saw his bloodline as an investment. They took his clan name. They took his language. They tried to turn him into a monster. And for a time, they succeeded. Spectators across the region came to know him by a title he despises: "The Butcher of Blackstone."

He wears an iron ring forged from the broken key to Stonejaw Pass — the only thing recovered from his father after the battle. Each morning, he grips the ring and repeats the oath he made as a child: "I will learn their names. I will find those responsible. And I will close the gate behind them."

Last memory before the experiment: "Some of your memories are blurry but you clearly remember feeling a need to be somewhere. During your travels you awoke one morning to a suspicious but promising letter next to your bed roll. 'If you want answers meet me here.' The letter contained a quick hand drawn map leading you to a small town not far from Waterdeep. Below the map the letter read, 'There is an old abandoned house near the edge of town. I am here as I write to you and I will be there every evening at sunset until you arrive.' You had your suspicions and you took your precautions but you knew your only option was to seek further answers so you went. The day you arrived you visited the abandoned house. Nothing odd or suspicious. You did find a desk with a stack of parchment and a dried up quill next to it in the shabby abandoned home. So you picked a corner and you waited to gain an advantage on this mysterious informer. You struggle to remember the rest but you recall hearing a knock at the back door of the home. You were watching the front windows intently at the time. You turned and repositioned yourself to see the back door then a flash filled the room. You remember a strange light then a ringing in your head. After that only darkness."
Vaelis Moonshadow
High Elf · Diviner / Cleric · Warcaster
Gift: Teleportation

A scholar who left Evereska for Waterdeep's criminal underground. A man accused of a theft he didn't commit, hiding in the woods, finding faith where intellect failed him. Knowledge is sacred. Every secret deserves uncovering.

  • Blind Jump Teleport yourself and one willing creature up to 60 feet as a bonus action. Landing inside an object deals 1d6 and returns you to origin. Recharges on short rest
  • Veiled Offering Teleport an item from your possession to any visible position within 100ft as a bonus action. Recharges on short rest

Early sign: You feel a vibration in your palms. For just a moment, you see your hands in two places at once — as if there's a delay in reality itself.

Born in the secluded elven city of Evereska, where bladesinging is both an art and a sacred tradition. His family expected a scholar. He found the rigid traditions of elven society suffocating — and left for Waterdeep, where his talents proved valuable to people operating outside the law.

He found himself working for a smuggling ring connected to the infamous Zhentarim. Five years ago he took a contract — a simple retrieval. An ancient elven relic hidden in the ruins of a forgotten tower. The job went terribly wrong. His crew was slaughtered. The relic vanished. Someone powerful blamed Vaelis. He didn't take it. But proving that may be impossible.

The Zhentarim want answers. Elven authorities want the relic recovered. Several powerful individuals believe he stole it. For months, Vaelis has been hiding in the woods, living like a hermit — watching, waiting for the right time to come forth and clear his name.

In the abandoned cabin, he began reading books stolen years ago but never opened. Religious texts. Ancient philosophical works. Among them he found writings devoted to Oghma — the Binder of What Is Known. Not because Oghma promised power, but because Oghma taught that knowledge itself was sacred. Every mystery deserved pursuit. Every secret deserved uncovering. Even uncomfortable truths. Especially those.

Last memory before the experiment: "You didn't think it was important at the time but now it seems to linger in your mind. Two days prior while foraging you found an abandoned fire pit near the creek closest to your home. The pit was about three miles downstream but you distinctly remember the ash bed in the pit feeling warm as you held your hand above it. One thing you do remember about your last evening home is that while you were meditating on your past in the deep hours of the night you were pulled out of your memories by an intense light that kept you blinded and reeling. You recall the sound of a thud which seemed like it was in the distance. Then warmth, comfort, a strange sensation of detachment from the waking world."
Voss Angmar
Half-Elf · Moon Druid · Fey Touched
Gift: Shifter

Witness to endings. Ten years watching the Gloamwood die, honoring its last breath. Now he walks the wider world for the first time in a decade — ensuring nothing passes from it unremembered. His druidic focus is the skull of his only companion.

  • Formless Take the form of any humanoid or inanimate object of similar size you've been within 60ft of for up to 1 hour. Recharges on long rest
  • Polyform Alter up to one fifth of your body into solid shapes — blades, clubs, plants, metals, rocks — for up to 2 minutes. Recharges on long rest

Early sign: A dry, itchy feeling runs over you — skin suddenly too tight. Looking closely at your pores you see the faintest shimmer. Tiny crystals, pushing to the surface when you tense up.

For the last ten years, Voss Angmar served the Circle of Dusk in the Gloamwood — an ancient forest nearing the end of its life. While other druids fought to preserve nature, Voss was sent to witness its passing. The Circle teaches that all things die, and that every ending deserves reverence.

Over the years, Voss watched the Gloamwood wither. Streams dried. Great trees fell. Wildlife vanished. His only companion was a tressym named Whisperwing, whose skull he now carries as his druidic focus after the creature passed beneath the forest's oldest tree.

Eventually the final ancient tree withered, and the Circle declared the Gloamwood dead. Voss remained through its final season, honoring the forest's last breath before departing. Now, for the first time in a decade, he walks the wider world — drawn to forgotten places and fading things, seeking to ensure nothing passes from the world unremembered.

Last memory before the experiment: "You recall a flower — a rare flower, vivid and red. It was the end of spring and summer's sun was taking hold. You remember spotting a single, fairly wilted moon drop daisy. These rare, pale-gold flowers bloom only when bathed in the light of a new moon during early spring. This one had somehow survived. You stopped to appreciate its resilience and beauty, but also the finality of its near future. As you took a knee near the crooked flower you remember a flash of light, then another. After that, darkness and the feeling of floating on a cloud."
Lucia Devaux
Aasimar · Rogue Lv5 · Urchin Background
Gift: Light Manipulation

Raised by Baldur's Gate's most renowned thief. Abandoned by whatever celestial power planted a spark inside her, she chose the shadows over the light. They call her the angel of vengeance.

  • Nightfall Steal all natural light in a 50ft radius for up to 60 seconds. Your eyes absorb the light and emit a dull yellow glow — visible from 15ft in the magical darkness, 60ft in natural light. Inside the darkness you see perfectly; outside it appears as dim light. Recharges on long rest
  • Fading Light Steal the light from a creature's eyes within 30ft for 30 seconds. On a failed Wisdom save, darkness consumes their eyes and they are blinded. Recharges on long rest

Early sign: As light pours into the chamber, you have an extremely hard time keeping your eyes open. The light is intensely painful — like staring straight at the sun. You blink hard and the sensation fades. A single tear streams from one eye.

Lucia never knew her birth parents. She was found as an infant wrapped in a tattered blanket in the gutter near a church in the outer city of Baldur's Gate. The man who discovered her claimed a faint golden light surrounded her when he lifted her up. He kept her and raised her as his own — teaching her the ways of the streets. To be curious but cautious.

The most renowned thief in Baldur's Gate was Gideon Devaux. He raised her and gave her his last name. He called her his spark from the gutter. Thieves, beggars, smugglers, and runaways became her family. As Lucia grew older, strange things began to happen. Wounds would close with a touch from her fingertips. She felt a pull toward something greater — and hated it.

Whatever celestial being had planted a spark inside her had abandoned her long ago. So she had to stay strong. Lucia embraced the shadows instead of the light.

One night everything changed. A robbery gone wrong. Lucia and her crew attempted to rob a corrupt noble slumming it in their neighborhood. The noble's guards discovered them and slaughtered everyone. Lucia was cornered in a dark warehouse, where she watched her closest friend die trying to keep her hidden. Something inside her awakened. Black wings erupted from her back. Shadows poured from her eyes, mouth, and fingertips like smoke. The guards fled in terror. After that night, she became known throughout the underworld as the angel of vengeance.

The light inside her still calls. But so do the shadows she learned to survive in. She stays close enough to Baldur's Gate to return in times of need.

Last memory before the experiment: "You recall a strange figure that night — a hooded man cloaked in shadows. You remember wondering if he was part of Gideon's network. They had a certain cunning discretion about the way they moved. They seemed to pay you no mind so you remember paying little in return, but something about it now feels off. Vaguely, like a distant memory, you remember seeing them again but they were facing away from you. You recall approaching and laying a hand on their shoulder. Then a flash from behind you. After that, the feeling of taking flight."
Lucien
Leonin · Echo Knight / Fighter · Mercenary
Gift: Self Manifestation

Sworn protector of the Sun-Chaser Pride — a pride now lost. He does not summon a magical clone. He calls forth the spectral remnants of his fallen kin. Every battle is fought alongside the dead. He is not one warrior. He is the last surviving body of an entire pride.

  • Duplication As a bonus action, create a duplicate of yourself within 15ft. It shares your stats, movement, and AC. It can take one weapon attack, bonus action or reaction — but no abilities, inventory, or spells. At the start of your next turn, choose which you occupy. The other becomes your Echo. Recharges on long rest
  • Reverberation As a bonus action, throw your melee weapon at a target within 60ft — without releasing it. A spectral duplicate flies through the air. On a hit, the target makes a Dexterity save (DC 10 + STR mod) or the weapon lodges in them: half movement speed, disadvantage on their next attack. Recharges on long rest

Early sign: You blink and for a split second you see yourself standing before you — clear as day, surrounded by a shimmering aura. Your reflection tilts its head in curiosity. You blink again and it's gone. A wave of nausea rolls through your stomach. Later, passing a rusty mirror in the lab, you catch a flicker — two reflections, but only one is looking back at you.

Born into the nomadic Oreskos plains, Lucien was a respected protector of the Sun-Chaser Pride — guardians of the Valley of the Echoing Roars, an ancient forbidden burial ground. Disaster struck during a massive raid led by Kelien the Soul Breaker, the Pride Eater. Trapped beneath a collapse of stone, he could only watch as his family was cut down. The sheer powerlessness broke his mind, triggering an uncontrollable Rage that let him claw out of the rubble. He was too late. The sole survivor.

Exiled by his own guilt, he wandered the borderlands as a lonely mercenary. But the spirits of his slaughtered pride refused to pass on while his heart beat with unavenged fury. One night, surrounded by bandits, he let out a desperate Daunting Roar. Instead of a mere sound, dust and shadows beside him coalesced into a towering spectral Leonin warrior — his deceased brother. Together they turned the tide.

He is not one warrior. He is the last surviving body of an entire pride. Every battle is fought alongside the spirits of those he failed to save. When enemies see him, they witness spectral lions appearing from dust, moonlight, and golden mist. Some are old warriors. Some are hunters. Sometimes a massive Leonin female with ceremonial armor appears — his mother.

When Rage begins: His eyes glow gold. Spectral lion spirits circle him. Ghostly roars echo across the battlefield. His mane moves as though caught in a supernatural wind. Enemies don't see a lone Leonin anymore. They see an entire pride charging behind him.

Last memory before the experiment: "You recall going to rest that evening with an uneasy feeling. Your pride was stirring inside your head and the echoes of their chatter kept you up late. Normally this isn't something you have to deal with but for some reason the spirits of your fallen pride felt uneasy, anxious even. The last part almost feels like a dream now as you reflect but after hours of laying awake trying to calm your fallen companions while also trying to keep yourself calm — because their fear made you fearful in turn — you remember finally finding sleep. After that you remember nothing."
The Seventh Subject
007
Subject Seven
Human · Aberrant Mind Sorcerer · 007

He was there before the others woke. The experiment affected him differently — accelerating and amplifying processes that remain, to this day, obscure. He has no recollection of any life before the Well.

He escaped before the rest of the party awoke from their slumber. He could be anyone. He could be anywhere. He is not an enemy. Probably.

  • Hesitant to TrustHe reached out to Roscoe. That wasn't an accident. But reaching out and revealing himself are very different things.

The Setting

Stonespire Island

Far west of the Sword Coast, beyond any chart or compass bearing, Stonespire Island does not exist — officially. It was raised and hidden by magical means over a long period, kept secret by layers of arcane concealment. No ship has found it by accident.

At its heart rises the Stonespire itself: a crystalline monolith of impossible height, jutting at an angle from the island's peak, catching the light in a way that no natural formation should.

The island holds dense tropical forest, open meadows, hidden caves, and beneath it all — a network of carved stone chambers built for purposes the party is only beginning to uncover.

"The meadow above feels like a breath of air. The earth below feels like a grave."

Threads & Hooks

What Remains Unknown

The party wakes with no memory of how they arrived. The answers lie ahead — and beneath.

  • The ExperimentWho conducted it? Why only these seven? The mind flayer parasite fragments suggest something was extracted, altered, or implanted. The gifts are not an accident — they were engineered.
  • The Changeling BodiesHundreds of numbered changelings in the mass grave below. Were they failed experiments? Sacrifices? The numbered tattoos are arcane — they mean something to whoever built this place.
  • The Island ItselfMagically created and magically hidden. This took centuries and enormous power. Someone wanted a laboratory the world would never find.
  • The GiftsThe party will first feel cursed, then confused, then slowly — terrifyingly — powerful. What was done to them? Are they weapons? Subjects? Survivors? Or something else entirely? Each bears an arcane tattoo behind the ear — three digits, 001 through 006 — that they don't yet know exists.
  • The Seventh SeatSix cradles. Six survivors. But seven tubes dropped to the floor. The seventh cradle sits empty. Who was 007 — and where did they go? Roscoe heard a voice in his head as he woke. That wasn't a dream. And the two dead researchers in lab coats? They have no visible injuries. They weren't killed by the experiment.
  • The Arcane SchoolsThe glyphs in the chamber correspond to known schools of magic — yet the combination is unlike any tradition. The experiment drew from all of them simultaneously.

The Schools of Magic — Weaponized

Abjuration. Illusion. Conjuration. Enchantment. Evocation. Divination. Necromancy. Transmutation.

Each school corresponds to a distinct color aura the party may begin to glimpse around themselves and each other. As the campaign progresses, the colors become more visible to those with arcane sight — and to those who built the island, a beacon.

DM Guide

Session One — Parable Seven

A full breakdown of Session 1 with read-aloud text, DM notes, skill checks, and discoveries. The party wakes with no memory, no answers, and gifts they don't yet understand.

Area 1

The Well of Seven

A strange humming sound overloads your senses. The vibration resonates in your body, startling you from a deep slumber. You open your eyes — vision blurry, the room ill-lit with very low visibility. It smells dank and cold, like dry sweat and fog. Light slowly filters in through a small circular hole in the ceiling.

As awareness returns and your vision sharpens, you realize you are restrained in a large metal cradle — arms, waist, ankles, and neck all strapped in place. Your gear and weapons are gone. You are all wearing the same dull beige cloth tunic and short pants in different sizes. The humming fades. More light pours in.

Lucia — as the light pours into the chamber, you in particular have a very hard time keeping your eyes open. It is intensely painful, like looking straight at the sun. You blink hard and the sensation fades. A single tear streams from one eye.

You all notice strange clear flesh-like tubes running along the ceiling in different directions, ending in what appears to be the fang of a serpent puncturing the front of your left forearm just below the elbow. All seven tubes run outward from the center hole, dropping down near cradles like yours, spaced evenly in a circle. In some of those cradles, shadowy figures are waking — taking in their surroundings just as you are.

Roscoe — as you come out of your blurry slumber you hear something. A nervously tense man's voice says, "I need to get out of here… they need to get out of here!"

The humming fades entirely. You feel a slight tug — then with a wet splat, all seven tubes drop through the hole and hang swaying in the center of the room, dripping a thick silver residue onto the immaculately carved stone floor. Strange runes and glyphs cover the floor where the light falls brightest. Behind each cradle sits a wooden chest. Your equipment — and your answers — wait to be found.

DM Notes The chamber is round with a slightly inclined ceiling rising toward the center hole (15ft diameter). The room is 60ft in diameter. The walls emit a faint silver/blue light. The wooden chamber door is locked from the outside. Each wooden chest behind a cradle contains that character's gear and weapons.

Skill Checks

  • DC 12 STRBreak the locked chamber door open (or deal 30 HP damage to it).
  • DC 10 PERNotice there are seven cradles but only six were occupied when the party woke. The seventh cradle sits empty — its tube lies on the ground beside it.
  • DC 12 PER / DC 10 INVThe leather straps on the empty seventh cradle are still buckled and looped as if something was strapped in and never released normally.
  • DC 13 ARCThe runes on the floor correspond to the eight schools of arcane magic — but their arrangement is unlike any tradition or formation you've encountered.
Potential Discoveries The silver residue from the tubes has no smell and doesn't react to fire or heat. A character who searches themselves carefully (DC 10 Perception) will find a small arcane tattoo behind their ear — three digits, 001 through 006. They are catalogued. They just don't know what that means yet.
Area 2

The Corridor

Outside the circular chamber stretches a long stone corridor carved from the same immaculate stone. The walls carry the same faint magical resonance as the chamber — humming just below the threshold of hearing. The corridor runs 100 feet ahead of you, 15 feet tall and 10 feet wide, lit only by the pale glow of the stone itself.

Halfway down on the right: an unlocked wooden door. At the far end: a spiral staircase descends into deeper darkness. Beside it, a short stairway climbs up to a wooden hatch above.

DM Notes The corridor is the hub connecting all remaining areas. The unlocked door (right, halfway) leads to Area 3 — the lab. The spiral stair (far end) descends to Area 5 — the mass grave. The short stairway (far end) ascends through a hatch to Area 4 — the surface meadow.
Area 3

The Strange Lab

The unlocked door opens into a chamber that stops you cold. One entire wall is lined with morgue drawers, floor to ceiling. Along another, operating tables are arranged in a row — some bearing draped bodies. The air smells of chemical preservation and something older. Cave earth. Copper.

In the far corner, an arcane desk sits beneath a lamp that flickers with a faint bluish light. Jars and vials crowd the surface — some containing what appear to be fragments of mind flayer parasites, some of those fragments whole and intact. A vial of dark, thick liquid that smells unmistakably of blood has been knocked over, its contents still spreading slowly across the wood.

On a metal tray: a scalpel and what appear to be tiny, carefully cut pieces of brain matter. A leather pouch holds worn surgical tools — some that once seemed arcane in nature, their enchantments now faded or broken.

Near the desk, toppled backward in a wooden chair, is a dead Githyanki in a lab coat. Near one of the operating tables, a dead changeling researcher lies facedown on the stone floor, cheek against the table leg as if they simply fell where they stood.

DM Notes Every body in the morgue drawers and on the tables is a dead changeling. Each has a five-digit number tattooed on the upper right arm near the shoulder, marked with arcane ink. The two researchers in lab coats have no tattoos, no visible injuries, and haven't been dead long. 007 killed them on his way out. His reasons are unknown. The party doesn't know this yet — and may never know, unless they piece it together later.

Skill Checks

  • DC 10 INVFind the medical journal on the desk. Among illegible formulas and anatomical drawings of multiple species, one entry reads clearly: "Subject 007 is responding to arcane resonance at an alarming rate yet he remains unstable. Studies are inconclusive. Remember to discuss this with Dr. Orwell when he decides to visit again."
  • DC 12 MEDThe two researchers in lab coats have been dead only a few hours. There are no visible injuries. No obvious cause of death.
  • DC 15 ARCThe number tattoos on the changeling bodies are arcane cataloguing sigils — sophisticated tracking and identification marks. Someone was keeping meticulous records.
  • DC 13 ARCThe mind flayer parasites in the jars have been modified. Something was extracted from them, or introduced into them. Their natural lifecycle has been deliberately altered.
Potential Discoveries A character searching the drawers will find all bodies numbered sequentially. The highest number found is in the thousands. Whatever happened here, it happened to a great many beings over a very long time.
Area 4

The Meadow — Surface

The wooden hatch opens into warm, humid air. You emerge through a large arched opening in a massive boulder. After the stone and shadow below, the sudden openness is almost overwhelming.

Not far ahead: a ring of smooth stone at ground level — the top of a large well. You recognize it. You were at the bottom of it.

Surrounding the well is a broad clearing of lush tropical meadow, impossibly vibrant. Beyond it in every direction: dense jungle, wall to wall, blotting out the horizon and the view of anything distant.

You tilt your head upward — and up, and further still — to the only landmark you can find. A massive stone spire shatters the jungle canopy and climbs thousands of feet into the air, piercing the clouds. It leans at a slight angle, catching the sunlight in a way no natural stone ever should. You are a very long way from anywhere you have ever been.

DM Notes This is a moment of awe, not threat. Let the players breathe. There are no immediate dangers in the meadow. The Stonespire should be described as genuinely disorienting in scale. The forest surrounding the meadow is nearly impenetrable without finding a path.
Potential Discoveries DC 12 Survival or Nature: The plant life in this meadow doesn't match any known ecosystem on the Sword Coast or Faerûn's tropical regions. Some species appear to be hybrids or magically cultivated. This island was not naturally formed.
Area 5

The Mass Grave

The spiral staircase descends for a long time. The smell reaches you before the sight does — death, deep and old, laced with incense that was never strong enough for the work it was meant to mask.

The staircase opens into a massive natural cavern. Your torchlight doesn't reach the far wall. What it does reach are the bodies — changeling bodies, hundreds of them, in various stages of decomposition, laid or fallen across the cavern floor. All numbered. All silent. The scale of what was done here is difficult to hold in the mind.

On the far side, a narrow cave mouth leads into deeper darkness. Following it, at its end, pressed against a fast underground river with ceilings too low to swim without going fully under, is a makeshift shelter. A cold fire pit with no ash. A bedroll. A small leather bag.

DM Notes The camp once belonged to a changeling test subject — mistaken for dead long enough to be thrown in the mass grave, who survived and hid in the caves. Their current whereabouts and condition are unknown. They were mapping the underground river system, looking for a way off the island. Whether they found one is a thread for later.

Skill Checks

  • DC 12 PERSpot the narrow offshoot cave mouth leading deeper into the cavern.
  • DC 10 INVThe fire pit has no ash — it was cleaned deliberately, not simply left unused.
  • DC 14 HIS / ARCThe squiggly lines on the back of the parchment are a crude but functional map of underground waterways — someone was tracking the river system beneath the island, searching for a route out.
Leather Bag Contents A small metal pot. A rusty knife and two-pronged fork. A piece of parchment bearing a charcoal sketch of a changeling woman with two children at her side. On the reverse: a network of connected lines mapping underground waterways.
Area 6

Edge of the Forest

Wherever the party attempts to enter the treeline, the forest resists. Vines thicker than arms, thorns layered across everything, root systems rising and tangling underfoot, canopy so dense the light becomes a rumor. Moving through this without a path would take hours to cover dozens of feet.

Near where they push in, however — a game trail. Narrow, worn, but clear. Something uses this path regularly. Shortly after taking to it, the underbrush goes quiet in a way that isn't natural.

⚔ Combat Encounter — Ogzul's Pack

The party is set upon by a pack of elemental dire wolves led by Ogzul — a dire wolf that escaped an experimental lab elsewhere on the island and bred an unusual pack. These are not normal dire wolves. Their coats carry elemental traits: stone-grey plating, frost-tinged fur, ember-lit eyes. They move with unsettling intelligence.

This is the only combat encounter in Session 1.

Skill Checks

  • DC 13 PERHear movement in the underbrush before the wolves emerge — the party is not surprised.
  • DC 15 NATThese aren't normal dire wolves. Their elemental mutations suggest exposure to the same arcane environment that pervades this island.
DM Notes This encounter should feel survivable but genuine. Ogzul acts last — watching, circling, evaluating. If his pack is sufficiently reduced, he withdraws rather than fight to the death. His intelligence is unsettling. He may be a recurring presence throughout the campaign, not simply a combat obstacle.
Area 7

The Abandoned Campsite

As night begins to settle over the island, the game trail leads to a small natural shelter — a low cave formed beneath a massive boulder set into the root system of a Kapok tree of extraordinary size. The tree splits above the roots into two great trunks that eventually rejoin into a single canopy far above. The cave is dry and sheltered, and has clearly been used before, though long ago. No supplies remain. But it is defensible, and the darkness outside is total. The choice is clear.

This is where they make camp. This is where the session ends.

DM Notes Let the party settle and breathe. Let them talk to each other — compare notes on the tattoos behind their ears, process what they saw in the mass grave, share what they know. Don't rush to the ending. Let it arrive naturally as Roscoe drifts toward sleep.
End of Session One

The Voice in the Dark

As you settle in and finally calm your nerves, your minds still race, making sleep uneasy. Just as you finally begin to drift into a slumber, you hear Roscoe speak softly.

Roscoe — as you begin to drift, a shaky voice cuts through the silence. Oddly, this voice sounds like an echo or a memory, and even though you have never heard these words before, you somehow speak them softly as you hear them:

"Good! You made it out of that place. Don't trust anyone! I saw what they were doing to them… I saw what they were doing to us. I…"

The voice stops.

DM Review

Session One — Rating & Notes

★ ★ ★ ★ ★ Premise

An exceptional foundation. The amnesia hook is familiar in tabletop RPGs, but what elevates Strange Times is the specificity of the horror underneath it — the numbered changeling bodies, the modified mind flayer parasites, the empty seventh seat. This isn't darkness without purpose. It hands players fragments and trusts them to want more.

Session Structure

Session One is deliberately light on combat — one encounter in Area 6 — and heavy on environmental mystery. This is a strong choice. Player energy is better spent processing what they see than fighting through it. The areas escalate in weight: the lab is unsettling, the mass grave is genuinely disturbing, the meadow is a breath of air, and the campsite is earned rest before the gut-punch ending.

Character Integration

The individual callouts — Lucia's reaction to the light, Roscoe's voice at both waking and sleep — are surgical. They give players a personal thread without locking anyone out of the central mystery. The gifts, discovered gradually rather than explained upfront, reward curiosity and create organic "wait, what just happened?" moments at the table.

Campaign Premise

Strange Times earns its name. A secret island built by magic, a facility running experiments the rest of Faerûn doesn't know exist, characters chosen for reasons they can't remember — and an NPC who got out first, whose nature as a changeling means he could be anyone. The arc ahead has genuine scope. The question of what was done to them, and why, is compelling enough to carry a full campaign.

Recommendation for DMs

Move slowly. Let the silence in the mass grave sit. Let Roscoe's player actually speak the words at the end. Don't explain the voice. Don't explain 007. The strength of this session is what it withholds. Run it like a horror film that hasn't decided what kind of horror it is yet.
