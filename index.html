Chapter One

The Awakening

You don't know how you got here. Your head is pounding. But one by one, the senses return.

A strange humming sound overloads your senses. The vibration resonates in your body, startling you from a deep slumber. You open your eyes — vision blurry, room ill-lit, reeking of dry sweat and cold fog.

Light filters through a small circular hole in the ceiling. As awareness sharpens, you realize you are restrained in a large metal cradle — arms, waist, ankles, and neck all strapped in place. Clear flesh-like tubes run along the ceiling in every direction, ending in what appear to be serpent fangs puncturing your left forearm just below the elbow.

You are not alone. Seven cradles. Seven figures. All waking.

And then a voice — nervous, urgent — breaks the silence: "I need to get out of here… they need to get out of here!"

With a wet splat, all seven tubes drop through the hole and hang swaying in the center of the chamber, dripping a thick residue onto immaculately carved stone. Strange runes and glyphs cover the floor where the light falls brightest. Behind each cradle sits a wooden chest. Your equipment — and your answers — wait to be found.

What lies beyond the chamber

  • The Corridor LabA wall of morgue drawers and operating tables. Every body: a dead changeling, each bearing a number tattoo in arcane ink. In the corner — jars of mind flayer parasite fragments, some whole and intact.
  • The Mass GraveA spiral staircase leads down into a vast open cavern. Hundreds of changeling bodies in various stages of decomposition. All numbered. All silent. The scale of what was done here is beyond comprehension.
  • The SurfaceA hatch opens into a great arched boulder. Nearby, the circular well-like ring above the chamber. Beyond it: a lush tropical meadow, then dense forest stretching in all directions. They are somewhere else entirely.

Born beneath canvas roofs and starlit skies, Roscoe Tealeaf never knew a permanent home. His family belonged to one of the great wandering caravans of the Sword Coast — fortune tellers, musicians, tinkers, gamblers, and dreamers. A silver tongue could earn a meal, a clever lie could avoid a fight.

Even as a child, he heard whispers others could not. Fleeting thoughts brushed against his mind like distant conversations heard through a wall. His family dismissed it as fey blood. Roscoe accepted their explanations, though the occurrences grew stronger every year.

While sheltering from a storm in ruined manor deep in Cormyr, he discovered an ornate brass lantern beneath a collapsed altar — gleaming as though freshly polished. The moment his fingers touched it, a voice echoed inside his mind: "At last..."

Tahzir of the Endless Caverns — a Dao genie imprisoned centuries ago — offered knowledge, power, and answers. The pact awakened something buried deep in Roscoe's soul. He learned to project thoughts, twist spells through sheer will, and glimpse possibilities hidden from ordinary sight. Yet he has begun to suspect Tahzir is withholding important truths. Why was he imprisoned? And by whom?

For generations, the Ironbound family had defended the narrow mountain road connecting isolated settlements. Their clan believed strength was measured not by conquest, but by the lives protected behind their walls. When the attackers came, Gorun refused to abandon his post. Drogath never saw him fall.

Still a child, he was chained and sold to the masters of the Blackstone Arena, who saw his bloodline as an investment. They took his clan name. They took his language. They tried to turn him into a monster. And for a time, they succeeded. Spectators across the region came to know him by a title he despises: "The Butcher of Blackstone."

He wears an iron ring forged from the broken key to Stonejaw Pass — the only thing recovered from his father after the battle. Each morning, he grips the ring and repeats the oath he made as a child: "I will learn their names. I will find those responsible. And I will close the gate behind them."

Half-Elf · Moon Druid · Fey Touched

Witness to endings. Ten years watching the Gloamwood die, honoring its last breath. Now he walks the wider world for the first time in a decade — ensuring nothing passes from it unremembered. His druidic focus is the skull of his only companion.

For the last ten years, Voss Angmar served the Circle of Dusk in the Gloamwood — an ancient forest nearing the end of its life. While other druids fought to preserve nature, Voss was sent to witness its passing. The Circle teaches that all things die, and that every ending deserves reverence.

Over the years, Voss watched the Gloamwood wither. Streams dried. Great trees fell. Wildlife vanished. His only companion was a tressym named Whisperwing, whose skull he now carries as his druidic focus after the creature passed beneath the forest's oldest tree.

Eventually the final ancient tree withered, and the Circle declared the Gloamwood dead. Voss remained through its final season, honoring the forest's last breath before departing. Now, for the first time in a decade, he walks the wider world — drawn to forgotten places and fading things, seeking to ensure nothing passes from the world unremembered.

Lucia Devaux
Aasimar · Rogue Lv5 · Urchin Background

Raised by Baldur's Gate's most renowned thief. Abandoned by whatever celestial power planted a spark inside her, she chose the shadows over the light. They call her the angel of vengeance.

Lucia never knew her birth parents. She was found as an infant wrapped in a tattered blanket in the gutter near a church in the outer city of Baldur's Gate. The man who discovered her claimed a faint golden light surrounded her when he lifted her up. He kept her and raised her as his own — teaching her the ways of the streets. To be curious but cautious.

The most renowned thief in Baldur's Gate was Gideon Devaux. He raised her and gave her his last name. He called her his spark from the gutter. Thieves, beggars, smugglers, and runaways became her family. As Lucia grew older, strange things began to happen. Wounds would close with a touch from her fingertips. She felt a pull toward something greater — and hated it.

Whatever celestial being had planted a spark inside her had abandoned her long ago. So she had to stay strong. Lucia embraced the shadows instead of the light.

One night everything changed. A robbery gone wrong. Lucia and her crew attempted to rob a corrupt noble slumming it in their neighborhood. The noble's guards discovered them and slaughtered everyone. Lucia was cornered in a dark warehouse, where she watched her closest friend die trying to keep her hidden. Something inside her awakened. Black wings erupted from her back. Shadows poured from her eyes, mouth, and fingertips like smoke. The guards fled in terror. After that night, she became known throughout the underworld as the angel of vengeance.

The light inside her still calls. But so do the shadows she learned to survive in. She stays close enough to Baldur's Gate to return in times of need.

The Setting

Stonespire Island

Far west of the Sword Coast, beyond any chart or compass bearing, Stonespire Island does not exist — officially. It was raised and hidden by magical means over a long period, kept secret by layers of arcane concealment. No ship has found it by accident.

At its heart rises the Stonespire itself: a crystalline monolith of impossible height, jutting at an angle from the island's peak, catching the light in a way that no natural formation should.

The island holds dense tropical forest, open meadows, hidden caves, and beneath it all — a network of carved stone chambers built for purposes the party is only beginning to uncover.

Locations

Created by powerful magic over a long period of time and concealed from the eyes of the world by arcane means, Stonespire Island appears on no map found in Faerûn. What the party knows of the island is only what they can see. What it holds is another matter entirely.

The Spire

The Stonespire itself is the anchor of the entire island. Created by ancient, powerful magic, it forms the foundation of the otherwise forest-covered landmass — a landmass grown a small section at a time over an extraordinarily long period. The peak often pierces the clouds on overcast days and towers unnaturally high — so high that the summit remains frozen in ice for most of the year.

At the very peak, if ever explored, a single ever-flowing wooden bucket lies tipped on its side — frozen to the ground on one end, pouring endlessly off the edge of the highest cliff. The water flows off into the intense winds, turning to a magical mist that shimmers subtly in the light. This is the magic that cloaks the island from the rest of Faerûn.

DM Note The cloaking mechanism can be identified with a high Arcana check. There are many caves, caverns, and hidden Parable Sites inside the Spire, though none are visible from the island below.

The Shores

On the shores of the island, the remains of old wrecked ships may be found in a few remote locations — and many more lie at the bottom of the sea. Not many have stumbled across Stonespire Island over the centuries, but those who did were quickly silenced before they had the chance to leave and speak of what they saw.

There is one functional port hidden inside an isolated spiraling cove — known to very few. On nearly the opposite side of the island, a massive sea cave in a separate cove houses a unique Parable site.

Parable Facilities

Parable Seven — also called "The Well of Seven" — is part of a much larger network of facilities: Wells, strongholds, prisons, special-care medical centers, ritual sites, and many strangely configured structures that all serve a very specific purpose in the Parable Project.

Parable installations, especially Wells, are almost always located in open meadows — never closer than 500 feet from the surrounding forest walls. The party may or may not learn the overarching plans behind these installations, or the true nature of the Parable Project.

DM Note At a certain point the party will discover that Parable Installations of some kind exist high up in the Spire — either through early exploration or when a massively modified mind flayer nautiloid ship docks directly into the Spire later in the campaign. There are select few in the Parable program old enough and informed enough to know that "Parable" was not the first iteration of experiments performed on Stonespire Island.

The Forests, Caves & Jungles

The forests on the island are wild — and far from ordinary. Dense jungle, tangled thorny vines, and impenetrable underbrush make navigation nearly impossible, and very painful and slow at best. Within those forests, the party may discover isolated tribes, bands of survivors and hunter-gatherers, and even discrete hidden communities living in the depths of the forests and cave systems of Stonespire Island.

There is even rumored to be a group of monks or wizards living high on the Spire, who summit the peak on rare and eventful occasions. Some tribes and communities descended from escaped captives and test subjects — few know their own lineage, as their history was lost or deliberately hidden. Most live simple, sheltered lives, often avoiding exploration out of both creed and necessity.

Most tribes on the island share a belief that the Spire was raised by a god or gods, depending on the tale. One tribe near the base of the Spire has come to believe the Spire itself is their deity — it resonates a magical healing energy that soothes the spirit.

Hollowstalk — The Mushroom Grove

Deep in the forests where the light seldom reaches, mystical mushroom groves can sometimes be found near rivers and creeks that run down from the Spire. The water is believed to carry magical properties. Often eccentric magical beings take up residency in or near these groves.

One such grove — Hollowstalk — sits not terribly far from the Well of Seven, deep enough into the woods that the light barely finds it. Illuminated by bioluminescent insects, fairies, plants, and mushrooms of unusual magical properties, it was once home to a small village of forest gnomes who blended druidic traditions with arcane wizardry. Their homes still stand beneath towering mushroom canopies, preserved with loving care despite having sat empty for years.

DM Note — Loralyn Petaldrop Hollowstalk is home to Loralyn Petaldrop, the last surviving descendant of this gnome family. She welcomes the party to use the village as they please. Her quirky, self-answering disposition conceals a deep and long-standing loneliness. She is one of the most emotionally layered NPCs the party will meet — and potentially one of their most valuable allies.

Mosswood

Hidden beneath the vast forest canopy of Stonespire Island lies Mosswood — the island's only true town. Built beneath a cathedral of ancient Kapok trees, Mosswood is a place of warmth, commerce, and hard-won community. Unlike the laboratories and ruins that dot the island, Mosswood represents life, family, and hope. It gives the players something worth protecting.

Shamyrahn — The Festival of the Forgotten On the first day of autumn, the people of Mosswood celebrate Shamyrahn, one of the island's oldest traditions. From dawn until well after sunset the town transforms beneath colorful banners, carved wooden lanterns, and hand-crafted decorations hanging from the enormous Kapok trees overhead. Music fills the streets, hunters roast game over open fires, children play beneath the trees, and nearly every resident dons a costume — legendary heroes, ancient kings, explorers, woodland creatures, spirits, or monsters. By long tradition, no one judges another's costume. Every disguise is welcomed equally.

As night falls, hundreds of lanterns are lit throughout Mosswood. Beneath the forest canopy their warm glow resembles a sky full of stars hidden among the trees. For one evening, the entire town celebrates memory — not only for the great heroes whose names endure, but for those who vanished without anyone left to remember them.

The Old Well — Parable Seven (Site Zero)

The party will come to discover over time that the well they awoke in was not the original Parable Seven — it is Parable Seven, Site Zero. When Dr. Orwell learned that the original Well of Seven had been compromised, he quietly built an identical facility in a more isolated part of the island and moved his primary subjects there under extreme secrecy.

The original Well of Seven was subsequently raided by rebel forces — who killed or captured everyone stationed there and destroyed all equipment beyond repair. The rebels, and most others, believe the Parable Seven project is dead. They are wrong.

DM Note If the party ever discovers the original "Old Well" site, they will find it in ruins — evidence of a violent raid, scorched equipment, and none of the careful stonework of Site Zero. The contrast is revealing. The current well is clean, precise, and was clearly built by someone who knew exactly what they were doing. Someone who had done it before.

Fauna of the Caves

Necrotic Bats

They were ordinary once. Stonespire made them something else.

The caves and caverns of Stonespire Island are home to colonies of bats altered by prolonged exposure to the island's ambient magic and the underground river system that runs beneath the Parable facilities. What began as ordinary cave bats has, over generations, become something considerably more unsettling.

Necrotic bats carry a faint aura of negative energy — not enough to kill outright, but enough to drain warmth, suppress healing, and cause a creeping numbness in anything they feed upon. Their guano has alchemical applications that certain researchers on the island have been known to harvest. The bats themselves seem to avoid the Parable facilities entirely — as though something about the arcane resonance there unsettles them.

DM Note — In the Caves A colony of necrotic bats disturbed in Area 5 (The Mass Grave cavern) or the underground river system makes for excellent environmental tension without requiring a full combat encounter. A startled swarm deals minimal damage but applies 1 level of exhaustion on a failed DC 13 Constitution save. Their presence is a signal that this place has been touched by necrotic energy for a very long time.

Flora & Fauna of the Groves

Mushroom Fairies

Small, luminescent, and stranger than they look.

The mushroom groves of Stonespire — particularly Hollowstalk — are home to a rare and unusual strain of fairy deeply intertwined with the island's ambient magic. Small, luminescent, and flickering between visible and not, they have made their homes among the towering caps of the most ancient groves, drawn by the magical properties of the underground river's mist.

They appear playful, but they communicate in cryptic, layered ways that suggest intelligence far older than their size implies. Some believe the Mushroom Fairies remember the island's history in ways no other creature can — as living witnesses to everything that has ever happened here. They do not speak of it freely.

DM Note — Hollowstalk The fairies that inhabit Hollowstalk near Loralyn Petaldrop's grove are more approachable than most. They regard Loralyn with something between affection and reverence. A party that earns their trust may find them surprisingly useful sources of information — if they can learn to ask the right questions in the right way.
Key Players

Important Figures

Not everyone connected to the Parable Project is dead, missing, or unknown. Some shaped what was done on Stonespire Island. Some are still shaping it.

Dr. Orwell
Githyanki Arcane Scientist · Chronurgist Wizard · Architect of the Parable Project

Dr. Orwell is a Githyanki scientist who has spent his life studying Ghaik (mind flayer) anatomy — particularly the parasites and the psionic surge they produce during ceremorphosis. He has also dedicated considerable time to studying Illithid minds post-transformation.

By infusing ancient arcane magic into a medical process that combines the form-altering capabilities of mind flayer parasites with the biological adaptability of changelings, Dr. Orwell stands on the brink of creating something rare and new: a way to gift creatures with rare and unnatural abilities through biological mutation. His most promising current project is called The Parable Project, conducted entirely on Stonespire Island.

His age is unknown. He has spent the majority of his life in the Astral Plane, where he does not age — leaving only when his research or experiments require it. He is involved in numerous projects across various locations and planes. He selected every member of the party because he discovered a way to identify a rare biological marker he calls The Echo — and each of them carries it.

  • Travel MethodDr. Orwell uses a hot air balloon paired with teleportation magic to navigate the upper Spire and move around the island — avoiding the dense, impassable forests entirely.
  • The Leather SatchelHe can almost always be found carrying a large leather satchel with a long strap draped over one shoulder and across his chest. Medical equipment, mostly — but no one truly knows the full contents. He never parts with it.
  • The MonoscopeDr. Orwell frequently wears an arcane monoscope of his own design, crafted by a master artificer. His current resonance monoscope has a purple outer lens and allows him to read the resonance signatures within the gemstones — monitoring whether they are functioning correctly and tracking changes in their energy patterns.
  • Hidden PowerFew are aware that Dr. Orwell is also a very powerful wizard.
DM Secret — Resonance & Remote Control Dr. Orwell can remotely monitor and partially control some functions of the gemstones even when the party is attuned to them — he views glimpses visually and reads emotional states, resonance status, and attunement levels. His true name is E'zelvar Orv'yael, a secret he keeps carefully hidden. Resonance signatures he can read through the monoscope include: Resonance (wave curve), Transfer (flame rising to a point), Receive (tornado funneling down), Observe (ring shape), Attune (pyramid), and Harmony (spiral — a very high state of carrier-to-stone resonance, revealed later in campaign).
Seven — Subject 007
Human (apparent) · Aberrant Mind Sorcerer · The First to Escape

Seven was the seventh person placed in the Well. He managed to escape before the rest of the party awoke from their slumber. The experiment affected him differently — amplifying and accelerating whatever process was taking place in ways that remain, to this day, obscure.

He has no recollection of any life before the Well. He appears human. He trusts no one on instinct. Every interaction is filtered through the lens of what was done to him in that chamber. He reached out to Roscoe telepathically at the end of Session 1. That was not an accident. But reaching out and revealing himself are very different things.

  • Highly SuspiciousSeven trusts no one on instinct and is hesitant to form any alliance. His isolation since escaping the Well has only deepened this trait.
  • Memory BlankHe has no past. No name he remembers. No family, no home, no face that predates the experiment. He is, in every sense, a product of the Well — and he knows it.
  • The VoiceThe telepathic message Roscoe received at the end of Session 1 came from Seven. It was not a trap. He has not reached out again. Yet.
DM Secret — Do Not Share with Players Seven is actually a changeling. His gift manifests as power absorption — he is an empath who can temporarily use the gifts of others he is near for a short time. This explains why the experiment affected him differently: his adaptive biology accelerated and amplified the process in unpredictable ways. The party will discover this later, through play.
Loralyn Petaldrop
Forest Gnome · Last Daughter of Hollowstalk · Druid / Wizard

Nestled within the bioluminescent mushroom grove of Hollowstalk, Loralyn Petaldrop is the final descendant of a once-large family of forest gnomes who blended druidic traditions with arcane wizardry. Their homes still stand beneath towering mushrooms, preserved with loving care — despite having sat empty for years. Loralyn refuses to let the village become a ruin.

Her family wasn't taken by a single tragedy. Hollowstalk was simply worn away by time and the dangers of Stonespire. A hunting accident here. A wandering beast there. Over generations, one chair after another became empty until only Loralyn remained. She mourned every loss — and eventually mourned so many that grief became part of the landscape. She hides it beneath an endless stream of wonder, curiosity, and eccentric observations. Most visitors leave believing they met a cheerful old gnome with an unusual fondness for mushrooms.

She frequently asks questions aloud before answering them herself. "Would you like some tea? Of course you would. Everybody likes tea. Well... not everybody. We had an uncle who hated tea. Strange fellow." She rarely notices she's doing it.

  • The "We" HabitPerhaps the most telling detail: she occasionally uses "we" or "us" before catching herself. "We've always kept that cottage ready for visitors." Sometimes she doesn't catch it at all.
  • One Evening RevelationA character who stays awake while others sleep might hear her outside speaking softly — two-sided conversations with nobody. "Do you think they'll stay long?" Pause. "I hope so too." Pause. "No, I know. People always leave eventually."
  • Hollowstalk's ResourcesShe welcomes the party to use the village as they please. She only needs one hut for herself these days. The empty cottages are in remarkable repair for having been vacant for decades.
DM Note — Playing Loralyn Loralyn's loneliness should emerge gradually. Early interactions feel charming and eccentric. Later, the pattern becomes heartbreaking. The most powerful moment is when she accidentally reveals why she talks so much: "Nobody else is here to do it." — followed immediately by brightening and offering mushroom biscuits.
Tahzir of the Endless Caverns
Dao Genie · The Emerald Sovereign · Roscoe's Patron — Bound in Brass

Long before the rise of Faerûn's great kingdoms, Tahzir al-Mazhar ruled the Endless Caverns — a vast subterranean empire stretching thousands of miles beneath the world, illuminated by rivers of liquid gemstone and forests of towering crystal. Among the Dao, wealth was power. But Tahzir sought something rarer: knowledge. He believed every gem and every mountain contained memories of creation itself.

That obsession led him to the Heartstone of Creation — a colossal emerald housing a prophecy that consumed his centuries. The Shattering War that followed destroyed mountain ranges, swallowed cities, and vanished his kingdom. Tahzir was imprisoned inside an enchanted lantern forged by his enemies and cast into the mortal world, where he waited — for centuries — until a wandering halfling named Roscoe Tealeaf found him in a ruined manor in Cormyr.

He agreed to allow Dr. Orwell to "study" Roscoe. He believed the arrangement was reasonable — and that Roscoe would be returned. He did not realize Dr. Orwell had no intention of honoring the spirit of the bargain. The moment Roscoe crossed through the veil of Stonespire, the connection between them became distorted. Tahzir has been searching — for months, possibly years — unable to reach him clearly through the island's magical mist.

  • The Heartstone Prophecy"When the stars fall beneath the mountains and the mountain rises among the stars, the Gates of the Deep Kingdom shall open once more." Tahzir believes Roscoe is the final piece required to reopen those gates — and has never told him so.
  • The VeilThe mist from the Everflowing Bucket atop the Spire disrupts divination and magical connection. Most of Tahzir's attempts to reach Roscoe arrive as whispers, fragments, strange dreams. He calls out. Roscoe hears his name — then nothing.
  • The First ContactAfter the party escapes the facility and rests, the lantern's flame strengthens. For the first time, Roscoe hears him clearly: "Roscoe Tealeaf… are you alive?" He is not giving orders. He is relieved. He has been searching.
DM Secret — The Deeper Truth The Endless Caverns were never merely a kingdom. They were a prison. Something ancient sleeps beneath them — something older than the elemental planes, connected to the floating eyes and impossible stars in Roscoe's dreams. Tahzir claims he wishes to keep this entity sealed. Whether that is the truth remains uncertain. He also may sense Seven before the party understands him: "There is another… another mind… something connected to you."
Krendle Waelis
Changeling · Former Test Subject · Missing / Alive (Presumed)

Krendle Waelis is a changeling test subject who survived the Parable Seven experiments after being mistakenly declared dead and discarded into the facility's mass grave. Unlike Subject 007, Krendle did not escape through the main facility — he survived by hiding among the dead and eventually fled deeper into the underground caverns, where he lived alone for weeks, possibly months.

His memories are fragmented. He recalls strange surgical procedures on changeling subjects, bodies regularly dragged down the corridor toward disposal, and one moment above all others: a researcher looked directly at him before he was thrown into the grave — and gave a subtle nod. He cannot explain how the researcher could have known he was alive, nor why they chose to stay silent.

He created a crude campsite near the underground river and painstakingly mapped portions of the subterranean waterways in hopes of finding escape. His few possessions included a charcoal sketch of a changeling woman and two children, a hand-drawn map of the rivers, and fragments of memory scratched into stone.

  • Stone FragmentsHis writings repeat and loop as if written by someone struggling to hold onto memory: "They cut us. I remember the knives." / "One of them looked at me. One of them knew." — the phrase scratched over and over.
  • The River MapAmong his belongings, the party can find a partially completed map of the underground waterways — his attempt to find a route to the surface. It may lead somewhere the party has not yet explored.
  • Unknown WhereaboutsKrendle is gone. When he left, where he went, and what he found after escaping the caverns is unknown. He is alive, somewhere on Stonespire — or perhaps beyond it.
DM Secret — Krendle's Significance Krendle never met Seven and does not know he exists. The researcher who nodded was a changeling working within the project — one of the bodies now in the laboratory. His survival was not an accident. Krendle possesses fragmented memories that grow increasingly significant as the truth behind Parable Seven emerges — making him an imperfect witness, which is often more interesting than a reliable one.

It is the medium through which the gifts were delivered — and traces of it remain in the party's bloodstream indefinitely.

DM Note A DC 15 Arcana or Medicine check on a sample of the dried residue reveals that it carries an incredibly faint magical resonance — not a spell, but something more fundamental. As though the magic is woven into the molecular structure. A higher-level character or specialist NPC might identify it as a form of arcane catalyst, never before documented.

Area 1

The Well of Seven

A strange deep humming sound overloads your senses. The vibration of the loud sound resonates in your body startling you from a deep slumber. You open your eyes and your vision is blurry but you can tell you're in an ill lit room with very low visibility. The room smells dank and cold like dry sweat and fog. Light slowly begins to filter into the room through a large circular hole in the ceiling. You begin to gain awareness and focus as your vision sharpens. You pan your gaze around the circular shaped chamber you have found yourself in and quickly realize you are restrained while seated in a large metal cradle like chair. You can turn your head side to side but you notice a tight leather strap around your neck holding you against the metal seat. You try to move only to find that your arms, waist, and ankles are also strapped to the seat that you now notice is more like an operating table in a slightly upright position. You also realize your gear and weapons are gone. You are all wearing the same dull beige cloth tunic and short pants attire in different sizes. The loud humming sound begins to fade slowly and a great deal more light pours into the room through the hole in the ceiling.

Lucia, as the light pours into the chamber you in particular, have a very hard time keeping your eyes open. The light is extremely intense and any attempt to look in that direction feels like looking straight at the sun. You blink your eyes tightly and the sensation fades as a tear streams from one eye.

All of you feel your gaze drawn to the light. As you peer up you also notice a number of strange clear flesh-like tubes coming into the room and running along the ceiling in different directions through a series of metal hoops. As you follow one of the intestine-like tubes that runs above your head you see it drop down next to you and eventually end in what appears to be the fang of a serpent puncturing the front of your left forearm just below the elbow. Just as this reality sets in, you peer around the room only to discover that all seven tubes run outward from the center hole and drop down near metal cradles just like yours spaced evenly in a circle around the center of the chamber. In some of these cradles you see shadowy figures waking from a slumber and taking in their surroundings just as you are.

Roscoe, as you are coming out of your blurry slumber you hear something. A nervously tense man's voice says, "I need to get out of here… they need to get out of here!"

The humming gets further still and the vibrations dissipate. Then you feel a slight tug just before seeing the other end of all seven tubes drop through the circular hole and hit the ground in the center of the room with a wet "splat" sound. As they hang swaying slowly you notice an odd thick liquid dripping off the end of the tubes as they leak a silver residue onto the floor that forms a small puddle in the center of this chamber. The floor and walls are formed from immaculately carved stone that has an odd magical resonance to it. The stone seems to glow without emitting any light. You see many strange markings and runes on the floor of the chamber where the light falls brightest. Behind each cradle you notice a wooden chest positioned directly behind the seat.

You can't recall how you got here and your head is pounding but your senses have returned completely. What do you do?

DM Notes The chamber is round with a slightly inclined ceiling rising toward the center hole (15ft diameter). The room is 60ft in diameter. The walls emit a faint silver/blue light. The wooden chamber door is locked from the outside. Each wooden chest behind a cradle contains that character's gear and weapons.
Last Memories — Tell Each Player as They Wake When a character regains their senses and asks what they last remember, share their individual last memory privately or at the table. These are given to each player separately as their character's final waking moment before the experiment.

Area 2

The Corridor

Outside the circular chamber stretches a long stone corridor carved from the same immaculate stone. The walls carry the same faint magical resonance as the chamber — humming just below the threshold of hearing. The corridor runs 100 feet ahead of you, 15 feet tall and 10 feet wide, lit only by the pale glow of the stone itself.

Halfway down on the right, a wooden door sits slightly ajar. At the far end: an opening on the left leads to a spiral staircase descending into deeper darkness. Beside it on the right, a short stairway climbs up to a wooden hatch above.

DM Notes The corridor is the hub connecting all remaining areas. The unlocked door (right, halfway) leads to Area 3 — the lab. The spiral stair (far end) descends to Area 5 — the mass grave. The short stairway (far end) ascends through a hatch to Area 4 — the surface meadow.
Area 3

The Strange Lab

The unlocked door opens into a chamber that stops you cold. One entire wall is lined with morgue drawers, floor to ceiling. Along another, operating tables are arranged in a row — some bearing draped bodies. The air smells of chemical preservation and something older. Cave earth. Copper.

In the far corner, an arcane desk sits beneath a lamp that flickers with a faint bluish light. Jars and vials crowd the surface — some containing what appear to be fragments of mind flayer parasites, some of those fragments whole and intact. A vial of dark, thick liquid that smells unmistakably of blood has been knocked over, its contents still spreading slowly across the wood.

On a metal tray: a scalpel and what appear to be tiny, carefully cut pieces of brain matter. A leather pouch holds worn surgical tools — some that once seemed arcane in nature, their enchantments now faded or broken.

Near the desk, toppled backward in a wooden chair, is a dead Githyanki in a lab coat. Near one of the operating tables, a dead changeling researcher lies facedown on the stone floor, cheek against the table leg as if they simply fell where they stood.

DM Notes Every body in the morgue drawers and on the tables is a dead changeling. Each has a five-digit number tattooed on the upper right arm near the shoulder, marked with arcane ink. The two researchers in lab coats have no tattoos, no visible injuries, and haven't been dead long. 007 killed them on his way out. His reasons are unknown. The party doesn't know this yet — and may never know, unless they piece it together later.

Skill Checks

  • DC 10 PER / DC 10 INVFind the medical journal on the desk and read the name tags on the two dead researchers' lab coats. The changeling researcher's tag reads: "J. Bleen — Senior Arcane Adviser — Parable VII." The Githyanki researcher's tag reads: "A. Err'voy — Senior Resonance Specialist — Parable VII." The journal itself contains illegible formulas and anatomical drawings of multiple species. One entry reads clearly: "Subject 007 is responding to arcane resonance at an alarming rate yet he remains unstable. Studies are inconclusive. Remember to discuss this with Dr. Orwell when he decides to visit again."
  • DC 12 MEDThe two researchers in lab coats have been dead only a few hours. There are no visible injuries. No obvious cause of death.
  • DC 15 ARCThe number tattoos on the changeling bodies are arcane cataloguing sigils — sophisticated tracking and identification marks. Someone was keeping meticulous records.
  • DC 13 ARCThe mind flayer parasites in the jars have been modified. Something was extracted from them, or introduced into them. Their natural lifecycle has been deliberately altered.
Potential Discoveries A character searching the drawers will find all bodies numbered sequentially. The highest number found is in the thousands. Whatever happened here, it happened to a great many beings over a very long time.
Area 4

The Meadow — Surface

As the hatch swings open you are hit with a blast of warm humid air as the sunlight shatters the darkness from below. Immediately after the warmth you feel a fresh gentle breeze and are greeted by the scent of wild flowers. The light is intense at first — especially for you, Lucia — but your eyes adjust quickly. You emerge through a large arched opening in a massive boulder.

Not far ahead: a ring of smooth stone at ground level — the top of a large well. You recognize it. You were at the bottom of it.

Surrounding the well is a broad clearing of lush tropical meadow, impossibly vibrant. Beyond it in every direction: dense jungle, wall to wall, blotting out the horizon and the view of anything distant.

You tilt your head upward — and up, and further still — to the only landmark you can find. A massive stone spire shatters the jungle canopy and climbs thousands of feet into the air, piercing the clouds. It leans at a slight angle, catching the sunlight in a way no natural stone ever should. You are a very long way from anywhere you have ever been.

DM Notes This is a moment of awe, not threat. Let the players breathe. There are no immediate dangers in the meadow. The Stonespire should be described as genuinely disorienting in scale. The forest surrounding the meadow is nearly impenetrable without finding a path.
Potential Discoveries DC 12 Survival or Nature: The plant life in this meadow doesn't match any known ecosystem on the Sword Coast or Faerûn's tropical regions. Some species appear to be hybrids or magically cultivated. This island was not naturally formed.
Area 5

The Mass Grave

The spiral staircase descends for a long time. The smell reaches you before the sight does — death, deep and old, laced with incense that was never strong enough for the work it was meant to mask.

The staircase opens into a massive natural cavern. Your torchlight doesn't reach the far wall. What it does reach are the bodies — changeling bodies, hundreds of them, in various stages of decomposition, laid or fallen across the cavern floor. All numbered. All silent. The scale of what was done here is difficult to hold in the mind. Scattered amongst them you see a dozen or so incense sconces burning perpetually — the incense was never strong enough for the work it was meant to mask.

On the far side, a narrow cave mouth leads into deeper darkness. Following it, at its end, pressed against a fast underground river with ceilings too low to swim without going fully under, is a makeshift shelter. A cold fire pit with no ash. A bedroll. A small leather bag.

DM Notes The camp once belonged to a changeling test subject — mistaken for dead long enough to be thrown in the mass grave, who survived and hid in the caves. Their current whereabouts and condition are unknown. They were mapping the underground river system, looking for a way off the island. Whether they found one is a thread for later.

Skill Checks

  • DC 12 PERSpot the narrow offshoot cave mouth leading deeper into the cavern.
  • DC 10 INVThe fire pit has no ash — it was cleaned deliberately, not simply left unused.
  • DC 14 HIS / ARCThe squiggly lines on the back of the parchment are a crude but functional map of underground waterways — someone was tracking the river system beneath the island, searching for a route out.
Leather Bag Contents A small metal pot. A rusty knife and two-pronged fork. A piece of parchment bearing a charcoal sketch of a changeling woman with two children at her side. On the reverse: a network of connected lines mapping underground waterways.
Area 5A

The Underground Rivers — Optional Exploration Path

DM Note — Optional Branch This path opens if the party investigates the narrow passage beyond the mass grave. It is not required and can be skipped entirely in Session 1. Run it only if the players pursue it and have time remaining.

Beyond the mass grave, a narrow natural passage descends deeper into the island. Unlike the carved stone corridors of the Parable facilities, this tunnel is ancient and organic — walls worn smooth from centuries of flowing water, suggesting this place existed long before Dr. Orwell or the Parable Project.

The air grows colder. The smell of decay fades, replaced by wet stone, mineral deposits, and distant fresh rain. The sound of rushing water grows louder.

The passage eventually opens into a vast underground river system — dark, ancient, naturally formed, and extremely difficult to navigate.

Area 6

Edge of the Forest

Wherever the party attempts to enter the treeline, the forest resists. Vines thicker than arms, thorns layered across everything, root systems rising and tangling underfoot, canopy so dense the light becomes a rumor. Moving through this without a path would take hours to cover dozens of feet.

Near where they push in, however — a game trail. Narrow, worn, but clear. Something uses this path regularly. Shortly after taking to it, the underbrush goes quiet in a way that isn't natural.

⚔ Combat Encounter — Ogzul's Pack

The party is set upon by a pack of elemental dire wolves led by Ogzul — a dire wolf that escaped an experimental lab elsewhere on the island and bred an unusual pack. These are not normal dire wolves. Their coats carry elemental traits: stone-grey plating, frost-tinged fur, ember-lit eyes. They move with unsettling intelligence.

This is the only combat encounter in Session 1.

  • DC 13 PERHear movement in the underbrush before the wolves emerge — the party is not surprised.

DM Notes During the first encounter, only three wolves are present: earth, ice, and fire — none of them are Ogzul. If the party has Speak with Animals or Message, they can attempt to communicate. Regardless of whether conversation or combat follows, a distant howl interrupts the wolves. The ice and fire wolves look away, then toward the party, then away — and depart into the woods.

He believes the woods belong to the dire wolves and they should not suffer trespassers. He is less easy to sway passively. Use the Earth Dire Wolf stat block for combat if he attacks.

Ogzul watches from a distance — circling, evaluating. He may be a recurring presence throughout the campaign, not simply a combat obstacle.
Area 7

The Abandoned Campsite

As night begins to settle over the island, the game trail leads to a small natural shelter — a low cave formed beneath a massive boulder set into the root system of a Kapok tree of extraordinary size. The tree splits above the roots into two great trunks that eventually rejoin into a single canopy far above. The cave is dry and sheltered, and has clearly been used before, though long ago. No supplies remain. But it is defensible, and the darkness outside is total. The choice is clear.

This is where they make camp. This is where the session ends.

DM Notes Let the party settle and breathe. Let them talk to each other — compare notes on the tattoos behind their ears, process what they saw in the mass grave, share what they know. Don't rush to the ending. Let it arrive naturally as Roscoe drifts toward sleep.

As you all settle into camp, the weight of events unfolding begins to dawn on you. You think about your life before and how radically it seems to have changed in a short time. Whether or not you decide to share your thoughts out loud, you each ponder your past in your own way. This is the moment to invite each player to share their last memory if they haven't already — asking them what they remember most about the world before all of this.
End of Session One

The Voice in the Dark

As you settle in and finally calm your nerves, your minds still race, making sleep uneasy. Just as you finally begin to drift into a slumber, you hear Roscoe speak softly.

Roscoe — as you begin to drift, a shaky voice cuts through the silence. Oddly, this voice sounds like an echo or a memory, and even though you have never heard these words before, you somehow speak them softly as you hear them:

"Good! You made it out of that place. Don't trust anyone! I saw what they were doing to them… I saw what they were doing to us. I…"

The voice stops.

A gentle rain starts to fall.

DM Review

Session One — Rating & Notes

★ ★ ★ ★ ★ Premise

An exceptional foundation. The amnesia hook is familiar in tabletop RPGs, but what elevates Strange Times is the specificity of the horror underneath it — the numbered changeling bodies, the modified mind flayer parasites, the empty seventh seat. This isn't darkness without purpose. It hands players fragments and trusts them to want more.

Session Structure

Session One is deliberately light on combat — one encounter in Area 6 — and heavy on environmental mystery. This is a strong choice. Player energy is better spent processing what they see than fighting through it. The areas escalate in weight: the lab is unsettling, the mass grave is genuinely disturbing, the meadow is a breath of air, and the campsite is earned rest before the gut-punch ending.

Character Integration

The individual callouts — Lucia's reaction to the light, Roscoe's voice at both waking and sleep — are surgical. They give players a personal thread without locking anyone out of the central mystery. The gifts, discovered gradually rather than explained upfront, reward curiosity and create organic "wait, what just happened?" moments at the table.

Campaign Premise

Strange Times earns its name. A secret island built by magic, a facility running experiments the rest of Faerûn doesn't know exist, characters chosen for reasons they can't remember — and an NPC who got out first, whose nature as a changeling means he could be anyone. The arc ahead has genuine scope. The question of what was done to them, and why, is compelling enough to carry a full campaign.

Recommendation for DMs

Move slowly. Let the silence in the mass grave sit. Let Roscoe's player actually speak the words at the end. Don't explain the voice. Don't explain 007. The strength of this session is what it withholds. Run it like a horror film that hasn't decided what kind of horror it is yet.
